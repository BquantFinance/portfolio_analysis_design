ğŸ’¡ Understanding Portfolio Overlap

What is Overlap?

Overlap occurs when multiple funds in your portfolio hold the same underlying securities. This can happen when funds have similar investment strategies or target the same market segments.

Risk Considerations:
  • Concentration Risk: High overlap can lead to concentrated exposure to specific securities
  • Diversification: Excessive overlap may reduce the diversification benefits of holding multiple funds
  • Redundancy: Paying multiple management fees for similar exposures
Overlap Levels:

ğŸ”´ High (3+ funds): Consider if this concentration aligns with your strategy
ğŸŸ¡ Medium (2 funds): Monitor for unintended concentration
ğŸŸ¢ Low (1 fund): Unique position providing diversification

Strategic Overlap:

Some overlap may be intentional and beneficial. For example, multiple high-conviction managers selecting the same quality companies can validate investment decisions. Evaluate whether the overlap represents thoughtful concentration or unintended redundancy.
